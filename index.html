Projects

I’m currently developing an ultralight gaming mouse with a focus on minimal design, performance, and user experience. The project began using MicroPython on an RP2040, which allowed me to quickly prototype core functionality, but I later transitioned to Arduino C++ for greater control and efficiency. This shift gave me a strong understanding of SPI communication, as well as hands-on experience with microcontroller programming and hardware integration.

The initial prototype used an RP2040, but I’ve since moved to a Nordic chip to achieve higher polling rates and lower wireless latency, essential for competitive gaming. Throughout the design process, I sculpted a physical model by hand and then employed photogrammetry to create a precise 3D digital model, which I use to refine the design in CAD before 3D printing and testing.

Looking ahead, I am working on building a brand called DPIlabs, with the goal of making this ultralight, high-performance mouse accessible to other gamers. The project has been a major learning experience, teaching me about hardware-software integration, sensor communication protocols, 3D modeling, prototyping, and product design, while also giving me insight into the end-to-end process of turning an idea into a tangible product.
